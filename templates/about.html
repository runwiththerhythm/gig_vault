About Gig Vault

Gig Vault grew from a simple need: stop forgetting great nights out. Instead of another social feed, I wanted to create a private, durable archive that makes it effortless to log gigs and relive them beautifully inside your vault.

How & Why it was built

GigVault is intentionally experience-first: capture the essentials in seconds, then layer in media and links as you like. It’s for regular gig-goers and festival lifers who want their archive — not a follower graph.

Technical highlights

  • Django 4.2 LTS with Bootstrap 5.3 UI.
  • Postgres (Heroku) using dj-database-url and Whitenoise for static assets.
  • Images via Cloudinary (multi-upload widget); GigImage model enforces max one cover.
  • Forms: crispy-forms + crispy-bootstrap5 for consistent, accessible layouts.
  • Autocomplete: django‑autocomplete‑light (DAL) for bands/supports.
  • Rich text: django‑summernote for notes (with safe sanitization).
  • Venue Search: Mapbox Search JS Hidden fields: venue_name, venue_city, venue_country (+ optional address_text).
  • Media linking only: sYouTube links/embeds; no uploads to YouTube or setlists stored locally.
  • Privacy: single‑user diary model — no public sharing between accounts.

Roadmap

  • Festival child gigs; quick‑add flows.
  • Smarter YouTube surfacing on gig detail.
  • Optional import tools for older gig lists.
